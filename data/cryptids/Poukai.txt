				In Māori mythology, Pouakai, Poukai, Hokioi, or Hakawai is a monstrous bird that ate people.[1]
According to an account given to Sir George Grey, an early governor of New Zealand, Hokioi were huge black-and-white predators with a red crest and yellow-green tinged wingtips.[2]
In some Māori legends Pouakai kill humans, which could have been possible if the name relates to the Haast's Eagle given the massive size and strength of the bird (which is believed to have hunted Moa weighing up to 300 pounds (140 kg)). Such a creature could very well have killed and eaten humans, Haast's Eagles did not become extinct until several hundred years after the arrival of the Māori.
However, it has been ascertained that the "Hakawai" and "Hokioi" legends refer to sounds produced by Coenocorypha snipe – in particular, the extinct South Island species.[1][3]
